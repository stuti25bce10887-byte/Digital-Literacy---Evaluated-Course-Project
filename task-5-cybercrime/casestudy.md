Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

Phishing is one of the most common cybercrimes where attackers trick users into revealing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or OTPs. A typical phishing attack begins with a fake email or message that appears to be from a trusted source like a bank or online service.

The attacker sends a message claiming there is an urgent issue, such as account suspension or suspicious activity. The message contains a link that redirects the victim to a fake website designed to look identical to the original one. When the victim enters their login details, the attacker captures the information.

College students and online users are often targeted because they frequently use digital platforms and may not always verify links. The consequences include financial loss, identity theft, and unauthorized access to accounts.

In many cases, victims only realize the fraud after money has been deducted or accounts have been compromised. This highlights the importance of awareness and caution while interacting with online messages and links.
